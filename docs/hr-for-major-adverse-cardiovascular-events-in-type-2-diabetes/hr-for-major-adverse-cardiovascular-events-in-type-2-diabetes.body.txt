In type 2 diabetes, does the intervention compared with control change major adverse cardiovascular events? This synthesis aggregates 6 randomized trials drawn from the ClinicalTrials.gov AACT snapshot dated 2026-04-12. Trial-reported effect estimates were pooled on the log scale using a Paule-Mandel random-effects model with a prediction interval based on the t-distribution. The pooled HR for major adverse cardiovascular events was 0.93 (95% CI 0.87 to 0.99). Between-trial heterogeneity was I-squared 21 percent and the 95% prediction interval spanned 0.81 to 1.07. These registry-derived data are consistent with a modest association, though the certainty remains uncertain and warrants cautious reading. Interpretation is limited by reliance on a single registry snapshot and by the small number of contributing trials, and does not generalise beyond them.
