In heart failure, does mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist compared with placebo change cardiovascular death? This synthesis aggregates 2 randomized trials of mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist drawn from the ClinicalTrials.gov AACT snapshot dated 2026-04-12. Trial-reported effect estimates were pooled on the log scale using a Paule-Mandel random-effects model with a prediction interval based on the t-distribution. The pooled HR for cardiovascular death was 1.30 (95% CI 0.51 to 3.34). Between-trial heterogeneity was I-squared 75 percent and the 95% prediction interval spanned 0.00 to 24873.64. These registry-derived data are consistent with a modest association, though the certainty remains uncertain and warrants cautious reading. Interpretation is limited by reliance on a single registry snapshot and by the small number of contributing trials, and does not generalise beyond the included trials.
