In atrial fibrillation, does anticoagulant compared with control change all-cause mortality? This synthesis aggregates 7 randomized trials of anticoagulant drawn from the ClinicalTrials.gov AACT snapshot dated 2026-04-12. Trial-reported effect estimates were pooled on the log scale using a Paule-Mandel random-effects model with a prediction interval based on the t-distribution. The pooled HR for all-cause mortality was 0.89 (95% CI 0.83 to 0.94). Between-trial heterogeneity was I-squared 0 percent, the 95% prediction interval spanned 0.82 to 0.96, and Egger's test for small-study effects returned a p-value of 0.90. These registry-derived data are consistent with a modest association, though the certainty remains uncertain and warrants cautious reading. Interpretation is limited by reliance on a single registry snapshot and by combining trials of differing interventions and designs, and does not generalise beyond the included trials.
