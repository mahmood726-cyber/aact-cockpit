In heart failure, does the intervention compared with control change heart failure hospitalization? This synthesis aggregates 17 randomized trials drawn from the ClinicalTrials.gov AACT snapshot dated 2026-04-12. Trial-reported effect estimates were pooled on the log scale using a Paule-Mandel random-effects model with a prediction interval based on the t-distribution. The pooled HR for heart failure hospitalization was 0.87 (95% CI 0.78 to 0.96). Between-trial heterogeneity was I-squared 80 percent and the 95% prediction interval spanned 0.60 to 1.25. These registry-derived data are consistent with a modest association, though the certainty remains uncertain and warrants cautious reading. Interpretation is limited by reliance on a single registry snapshot and by the small number of contributing trials, and does not generalise beyond them.
