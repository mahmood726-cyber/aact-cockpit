Among anticoagulants for atrial fibrillation, which option ranks best for stroke or systemic embolism? This network meta-analysis connects 5 treatments across 8 randomized comparisons from the ClinicalTrials.gov AACT snapshot dated 2026-04-12. Trial contrasts were synthesised with a random-effects model on the log scale, taking warfarin as the network reference. By surface under the cumulative ranking curve, rivaroxaban ranked highest, with a pooled HR of 0.81 (95% CI 0.60 to 1.08) versus warfarin. Its ranking statistic was 84 percent, the network was connected, and rankings should not be read as head-to-head superiority across differing trial populations. Because the network forms a tree without closed loops, consistency cannot be assessed, so the indirect comparisons warrant cautious reading. Interpretation is limited by reliance on a single registry snapshot and by sparse direct evidence, and does not generalise beyond the included trials.
