In heart failure, is the accumulated randomized evidence on the intervention versus control for all-cause mortality conclusive? This analysis accrues 25 randomized trials from the ClinicalTrials.gov AACT snapshot dated 2026-04-12, spanning 2002 to 2021. A cumulative random-effects meta-analysis was monitored by trial sequential analysis using O'Brien-Fleming boundaries against a diversity-adjusted required information size. The cumulative pooled HR for all-cause mortality was 0.95 (95% CI 0.92 to 0.98). The monitoring boundary was crossed by trial 7, and the accrued information reached 542 percent of the required size. This pattern is consistent with a firm signal that is unlikely to be a chance finding, though the certainty remains observational and warrants cautious reading. Interpretation is limited by reliance on a single registry snapshot, by registration-time effect reporting, and does not generalise beyond the included trials.
