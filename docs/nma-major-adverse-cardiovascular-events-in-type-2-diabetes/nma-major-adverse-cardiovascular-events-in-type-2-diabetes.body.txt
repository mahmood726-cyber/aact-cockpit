Among anticoagulants for type 2 diabetes, which option ranks best for major adverse cardiovascular events? This network meta-analysis connects 4 treatments across 10 randomized comparisons from the ClinicalTrials.gov AACT snapshot dated 2026-04-12. Trial contrasts were synthesised with a random-effects model on the log scale, taking placebo as the network reference. By surface under the cumulative ranking curve, DPP-4 inhibitor ranked highest, with a pooled HR of 0.98 (95% CI 0.82 to 1.17) versus placebo. Its ranking statistic was 76 percent, the network was connected, and rankings should not be read as head-to-head superiority across differing trial populations. Because the network forms a tree without closed loops, consistency cannot be assessed, so the indirect comparisons warrant cautious reading. Interpretation is limited by reliance on a single registry snapshot and by sparse direct evidence, and does not generalise beyond the included trials.
