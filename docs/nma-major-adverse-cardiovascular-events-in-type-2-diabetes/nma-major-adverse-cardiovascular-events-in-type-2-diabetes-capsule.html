For each closed loop, direct evidence is compared with indirect evidence; an inconsistency factor (IF) far from 1 with a small p-value signals disagreement.

06b · Transitivity & class composition

Transitivity — clinical comparability across the network — is the assumption behind every indirect comparison, and is separate from the statistical consistency tested above. Each node may lump several distinct agents (a class-effect assumption), and edges may differ in era or trial size; those are effect-modifiers that can bias indirect estimates even when loops look statistically consistent.
