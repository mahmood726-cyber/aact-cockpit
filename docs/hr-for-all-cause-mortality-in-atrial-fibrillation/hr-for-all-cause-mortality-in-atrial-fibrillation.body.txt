In atrial fibrillation, does the intervention compared with control change all-cause mortality? This synthesis aggregates 11 randomized trials drawn from the ClinicalTrials.gov AACT snapshot dated 2026-04-12. Trial-reported effect estimates were pooled on the log scale using a Paule-Mandel random-effects model with a prediction interval based on the t-distribution. The pooled HR for all-cause mortality was 0.90 (95% CI 0.84 to 0.97). Between-trial heterogeneity was I-squared 61 percent, the 95% prediction interval spanned 0.75 to 1.08, and Egger's test for small-study effects returned a p-value of 0.93. These registry-derived data are consistent with a modest association, though the certainty remains uncertain and warrants cautious reading. Interpretation is limited by reliance on a single registry snapshot and by the small number of contributing trials, and does not generalise beyond them.
