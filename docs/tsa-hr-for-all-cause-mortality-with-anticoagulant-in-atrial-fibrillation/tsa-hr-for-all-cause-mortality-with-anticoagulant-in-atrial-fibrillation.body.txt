In atrial fibrillation, is the accumulated randomized evidence on anticoagulant versus control for all-cause mortality conclusive? This analysis accrues 7 randomized trials from the ClinicalTrials.gov AACT snapshot dated 2026-04-12, spanning 2006 to 2016. A cumulative random-effects meta-analysis was monitored by trial sequential analysis using O'Brien-Fleming boundaries against a diversity-adjusted required information size. The cumulative pooled HR for all-cause mortality was 0.89 (95% CI 0.83 to 0.94). The monitoring boundary was crossed by trial 2, and the accrued information reached 184 percent of the required size. This pattern is consistent with a firm signal that is unlikely to be a chance finding, though the certainty remains observational and warrants cautious reading. Interpretation is limited by reliance on a single registry snapshot, by registration-time effect reporting, and does not generalise beyond the included trials.
