Among anticoagulants for advanced melanoma, which option ranks best for overall survival? This network meta-analysis connects 6 treatments across 8 randomized comparisons from the ClinicalTrials.gov AACT snapshot dated 2026-04-12. Trial contrasts were synthesised with a random-effects model on the log scale, taking dacarbazine as the network reference. By surface under the cumulative ranking curve, dabrafenib ranked highest, with a pooled HR of 0.26 (95% CI 0.12 to 0.56) versus dacarbazine. Its ranking statistic was 98 percent, the network was connected, and rankings should not be read as head-to-head superiority across differing trial populations. Loop inconsistency testing across 1 closed loop or loops showed no significant inconsistency at a smallest p-value of 0.40, though certainty stays observational and warrants cautious reading. Interpretation is limited by reliance on a single registry snapshot and by sparse direct evidence, and does not generalise beyond the included trials.
