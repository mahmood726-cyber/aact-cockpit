Across the ClinicalTrials.gov registry, how often do reported treatment-effect analyses reach statistical significance, and does this vary by sponsor and trial size? This analysis characterises 32,307 ratio-measure analyses, hazard, odds, and risk ratios, from 5,136 trials in the AACT snapshot dated 2026-04-12. Each analysis was classed as significant when its 95% confidence interval excluded the null of one, and effects were summarised on the log scale by lead-sponsor class and enrolment. 36.8 percent of analyses were statistically significant, with a median effect of 1.07. Industry-sponsored analyses reached significance more often (40.0 versus 26.2 percent), and smaller trials reported larger effects. These descriptive patterns reflect what trialists registered and reported, not the truth of any single treatment, and warrant cautious reading. Interpretation is limited to ratio analyses with usable intervals, mixes heterogeneous measures and outcomes, and does not adjust for multiplicity or risk of bias.
