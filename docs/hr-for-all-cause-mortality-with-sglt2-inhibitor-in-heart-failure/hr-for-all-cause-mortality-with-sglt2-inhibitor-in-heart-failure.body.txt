In heart failure, does SGLT2 inhibitor compared with placebo change all-cause mortality? This synthesis aggregates 4 randomized trials of SGLT2 inhibitor drawn from the ClinicalTrials.gov AACT snapshot dated 2026-04-12. Trial-reported effect estimates were pooled on the log scale using a Paule-Mandel random-effects model with a prediction interval based on the t-distribution. The pooled HR for all-cause mortality was 0.93 (95% CI 0.86 to 1.00). Between-trial heterogeneity was I-squared 4 percent, the 95% prediction interval spanned 0.81 to 1.06, and Egger's test for small-study effects returned a p-value of 0.51. These registry-derived data are consistent with a modest association, though the certainty remains uncertain and warrants cautious reading. Interpretation is limited by reliance on a single registry snapshot and by the small number of contributing trials, and does not generalise beyond the included trials.
